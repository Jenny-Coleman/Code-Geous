History of Mzamomtsha Primary School

The uprising of Mzamomtsha

Mzamomtsha Primary was a fresh start for the community and their school building project created by SAOTA. this was a part of a school building that focused on the marginalized area affected by the Apartheid, this involved rapid urbanization and urban migration.With all these sudden changes occuring; the opportunity to grow the area was taken.A school was built, town hall, civic centre sports centre, social and economic growth/opportunities.



The small town that Mzamomtsha Primary belongs to, grew and was split in 3 sections:

  1. A section belongs to the children
  2. A section belongs to those that belongs to the community and what the community can stake claim to.
  3. The last section was comprised of public facilities such as the sport centres(A physical training slab), bascet ball corts and a liabrary


The buildings represents the adult and the outside world and has a confident, sophisticated language direct counter to the "twee".

The basis around the once normal urban school, changed so much over the years that urbanisation took place in a way that provided the surrounding areas with joibs in building to build new facilitis for the growth of the town itself.
