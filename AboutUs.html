About Mzamomtsha Primary School

Mzamomtsha Primary School in Mfuleni, Khayelitsha, Western Cape

Mzamomtsha Primary School is a public primary school located at 49 Valangentambo Street. It is one of the public primary schools in urban Driftsands suburb, City Of Cape Town in Western Cape Province, South Africa.

It was a part of a school building programme that focused on a marginalized area affected by apartheid, rapid urbanization and urban migration. As Greg Truen, Project Partner explains, the growing population can be empowered within such a model, and transform itself into part school, part town hall, part civic centre and part sports centre that provide social and economic opportunity.

Our vision at Mzamomtsha Primary School is to empower students to acquire, demonstrate, articulate and value knowledge and skills that will support them, as life-long learners, to participate in and contribute to the global world and practice the core values of the school: respect, tolerance & inclusion, and excellence.Our mission is to develop students with active and creative minds, a sense of understanding and compassion for others, and the courage to act on their beliefs. We stress the total development of each child: spiritual, moral, intellectual, social, emotional, and physical.The School provides facilities in an impoverished and marginalized area on the Cape Flats. The school has sports fields, a library, specialist classrooms and a computer room.
